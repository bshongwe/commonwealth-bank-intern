Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Password Security:

1. Create Strong Passwords: Use complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols to make it harder for hackers to guess.
2. Avoid Reusing Passwords: Each online account should have a unique password. Reusing passwords increases the risk of multiple accounts being compromised if one is breached.
3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adding an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification, such as a code sent to your phone, significantly reduces the chances of unauthorized access.

Secure Communication:

1. Use Secure Networks: Avoid accessing sensitive banking information on public Wi-Fi networks. Use secure, password-protected networks or a virtual private network (VPN) for added security.
2. Encrypt Emails: When communicating with your bank via email, ensure that sensitive information is encrypted to prevent interception by unauthorized parties.

Monitoring and Alerts:

1. Regularly Monitor Accounts: Check your bank statements and online transactions frequently for any unauthorized activity. Promptly report any discrepancies to your bank.
